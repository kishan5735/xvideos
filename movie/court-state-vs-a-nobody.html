Court: State vs A. Nobody – Movie Review, Cast, and Highlights

Court: State vs A. Nobody is a critically acclaimed Indian courtroom drama directed by Chaitanya Tamhane. Released in 2014, the film offers a realistic and deeply moving portrayal of the legal system through the trial of a folk singer accused of abetting a man’s death.

Quick Details

  • Title: Court: State vs A. Nobody
  • Language: Marathi, Gujarati, Hindi, and English
  • Genre: Drama, Courtroom
  • Director: Chaitanya Tamhane
  • Release Year: 2014
  • Country: India

Plot Summary

The film follows the legal proceedings against Narayan Kamble, an aging folk singer who is accused of inciting a sewage worker’s suicide through his protest song. The story explores how different perspectives, from lawyers to judges, shape the case as it drags on in a slow, bureaucratic system. It’s not just a legal story—it’s a look at modern society, justice, and freedom of expression.

Cast

  • Vira Sathidar as Narayan Kamble
  • Vivek Gomber as Advocate Vinay Vora
  • Geetanjali Kulkarni as Public Prosecutor Nutan
  • Pradeep Joshi as Judge Sadavarte

Awards and Recognition

  • National Film Award for Best Feature Film (India)
  • Best Film – Horizons, Venice Film Festival
  • India’s official entry to the 88th Academy Awards

Court is not a typical courtroom drama. It’s slow, thoughtful, and incredibly real. If you enjoy socially relevant films that make you think, this is a must-watch.
